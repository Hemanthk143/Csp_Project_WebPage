History of Rabbits

Rabbits (Oryctolagus cuniculus) have a long history with humans, primarily as a source of food and fur, and more recently as beloved pets. Domestication of rabbits is thought to have begun during the Roman Empire, when they were kept in fenced areas to be farmed for meat. Monks in medieval Europe are credited with selectively breeding rabbits for various traits, leading to the development of different breeds.

In the 19th century, rabbits began to be kept as pets in Europe and North America, and various breeds were developed with distinct characteristics in size, fur type, and color. Today, rabbits are popular pets known for their gentle nature, and they continue to be valued for their contributions to agriculture and research.

Breeds of Rabbits

Netherland Dwarf

  • Origin: Netherlands
  • Colors: Variety of colors including black, blue, chocolate, and lilac
  • Size: Small
  • Weight: 1-2.5 lbs
  • Lifespan: 10-12 years
  • Temperament: Lively, energetic, and sometimes feisty
  • Usage: Popular as a pet and show rabbit

Flemish Giant

  • Origin: Belgium
  • Colors: Sandy, fawn, white, light gray, steel gray, black, and blue
  • Size: Large
  • Weight: 15-20 lbs or more
  • Lifespan: 5-8 years
  • Temperament: Gentle, calm, and friendly
  • Usage: Popular as a pet, show rabbit, and meat rabbit

Habits and Food

In our research, we found that rabbits require a balanced diet that includes hay, fresh vegetables, and a small amount of pellets. Hay is essential for their digestive health and helps to wear down their constantly growing teeth. Fresh vegetables such as leafy greens provide necessary vitamins and minerals. We advised the farm staff on the importance of a balanced diet and proper feeding routines to ensure the rabbits' health and vitality.

Diseases and Preventions

Rabbits can be affected by various diseases, but here are a few common ones and their preventions:

Myxomatosis: A viral disease spread by fleas and mosquitoes. Prevention includes vaccination and controlling flea populations.
Rabbit Hemorrhagic Disease (RHD): A highly contagious viral disease. Prevention involves vaccination and good hygiene practices.
Pasteurellosis (Snuffles): A bacterial infection causing respiratory issues. Preventive measures include maintaining clean living conditions and reducing stress.
Ear Mites: Parasitic mites that infest the ears. Prevention involves regular health checks and maintaining good hygiene.
