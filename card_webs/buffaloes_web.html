History of Buffaloes

Buffaloes were first tamed in Mesopotamia around 2500 B.C. during the Akkadian dynasty. They were also domesticated in the Indus Valley civilization, extending to parts of what is now India and Pakistan. By 2500 B.C., river buffaloes were present in India, and by 1000 B.C., in Mesopotamia. Swamp-type buffaloes originated in China with a domestication history of about 4000 years. River-type buffaloes have their origins in India, with a domestication history of around 5000 years. In the Indian subcontinent, buffaloes were well domesticated by the medieval period. They were considered milch animals along with cows, goats, and sheep since ancient times. The American bison, often called buffalo, is native to North America and is a different species. Today, buffaloes are vital for agriculture and dairy production in many parts of the world.

Breeds of Buffaloes

Murrah

  • Also known as: Delhi, Kundi, Kali
  • Home: Rohtak, Hisar and Sind of Haryana, Nabha and Patiala districts of Punjab and Southern parts of Delhi
  • Color: Usually jet black with white markings on the tail, face and extremities
  • Milk yield: 1,500-2,500 kg per lactation
  • Age at first calving: 45-50 months in villages, 36-40 months in good herds
  • Efficiency: Most efficient milk and butterfat producer in India

Jaffrabadi

  • Heaviest breed: Heaviest Indian breed of buffalo
  • Found in: Gir forests, Kutch, and Jamnagar districts of Gujarat
  • Color: Usually black with a long body and heavy horns
  • Average milk yield: 1000-1200 kg per lactation
  • Uses: Bullocks are heavy and used for plowing and carting

Habits and Food

Buffaloes are known for their robust digestive systems, allowing them to process tough, fibrous plant material. They primarily consume grasses, but their diet can also include herbs, tree leaves, and in scarce times, even mosses and tree bark. Buffaloes require a significant amount of water and will often be found near water sources. They spend a large portion of their day grazing and are constantly on the move to find fresh patches of grass. Their adaptability in diet is crucial for survival, especially during droughts when they switch from grasses to shrubs or aquatic plants. This dietary flexibility helps maintain their energy levels and supports their large size and strength .

Buffaloes Diseases and Prevention

Diseases:

  • Foot-and-Mouth Disease (FMD): Highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-hoofed animals.
  • Tuberculosis (TB): Bacterial infection primarily affecting the lungs but can spread to other parts of the body.
  • Brucellosis: Bacterial infection causing reproductive issues and reduced milk production.
  • Bovine Viral Diarrhea (BVD): Viral disease affecting the immune system and reproductive system.
  • Mastitis: Inflammation of the udder tissue caused by bacterial infection.

Prevention Measures:

  • Comprehensive Vaccination Programs: Protect against diseases like FMD, anthrax, brucellosis, and bovine tuberculosis.
  • Proper Hygiene and Sanitation: Clean conditions are crucial for disease prevention.
  • Nutrition and Balanced Diet: Supports immune system health.
  • Veterinary Care and Regular Monitoring: Early detection and treatment of diseases.
  • Biosecurity Practices: Prevent the introduction and spread of diseases.
  • Disease Surveillance and Record Keeping: Track health data for timely interventions.
